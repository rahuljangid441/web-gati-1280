JOGIGHOPA MULTIMODAL LOGISTICS PARK

The National Master Plan helped plan a MultiModal Logistics Park at the intersection of national waterways, rail network and roadways, connected to the rest of India and supplemented by a 4 lane bridge across the Brahmaputra river, drastically reducing the existing travel distance and time.

Overall project scope

a ~200-acre land parcel transferred by Ashok Paper Mill and an additional ~80-acre land parcel acquired for external connectivity.

Expected completion date:

March 2024

Cost outlay:

Rs. 1,102 crores ($162 million)

BACKGROUND AND VISION FOR THE PROJECT

On the northern banks of the beautiful river Brahmaputra, in the district of Bongaigaon in Assam, this ambitious project will be the country’s first Multi Modal Logistics Park (MMLP), which will enable critical, seamless and multimodal transport of goods to and from the Northeast of India across road, rail and waterways.

The location will act as an aggregation point and enable the long-distance linehaul to move on more efficient modes such as rail, waterways, and trucks with higher carrying capacity while ensuring that the first mile and last mile movement within the Northeast region will be on medium and smaller-sized trucks.

This will boost the region's economic development and improve connectivity with the entire nation. The park will have a variety of facilities, including warehouses, cold storage facilities, and cargo terminals to facilitate and allocate the goods movement among different modes of transport. The development of state-of-the-art warehouses and cold storage facilities shall cater to a diverse array of goods, including agricultural produce, fisheries output, and processed commodities that have the potential to boost agriculture, fisheries, and industries.

It will enable the reduction of both logistics costs and vehicular emissions from freight transmissions. It will also connect to the economic node of the fishing cluster at Goalpara and a logistics node at Jogighopa railway station and boost trade in the region significantly. The completed project will also include the longest river bridge connecting flood-prone areas.

This also acts as a possible link to neighbouring countries like Bhutan, Bangladesh and Myanmar in terms of regional cooperation and trade prospects in the future.

THE GATISHAKTI EDGE AND APPROACH

Before the PM GatiShakti approach, the need for comprehensive connectivity and efficient logistics in the Northeast region was evident. The region's potential faced limitations due to fragmented transport systems, inadequate facilities, and constrained trade prospects. The absence of an integrated approach was evident in the disconnect between the fishing cluster in Goalpara and the rest of the trade ecosystem, highlighting the challenges faced by the region.

The transformation of the MMLP at Jogighopa began with the integration of PM GatiShakti's principles. This involved harnessing data layers from diverse Ministries, Departments, and States along with social, ecological and topographical data from the NMP platform .
By leveraging this amalgamation of information, a holistic view of the area development approach emerged, incorporating economic nodes, railway networks, airports, ports, waterways, schools, hospitals, and ecological considerations.

The integration of economic nodes with strategic planning through PM GatiShakti approach yielded unparalleled connectivity between the Jogighopa railway station and the Goalpara fishing cluster. Along with that, it also enabled synergy with the national highway infrastructure in Assam, including the Jogighopa Bridge parallel to Naranarayan Setu on NH-17 as well as the Dhubri-Phulbari Bridge on NH-127B.

MULTIMODAL CONNECTIVITY PLAN

With PM GatiShakti at the helm, the MMLP project took a transformative trajectory. The forthcoming Dhubri-Phulbari Bridge epitomises this transformation, defying the challenges of disconnected flood-prone areas. The 4-lane bridge's design, is set to reduce the distance between Dhubri and Phulbari by a staggering 90%, reducing the current ~205 km journey to a mere ~20 km. This monumental bridge is poised to be India's longest, an emblem of ambitious infrastructure development.

Through NMP platform, it was found that external road connectivity aligned with a railway intersection. This called for a Road Under Bridge (RUB) for seamless connectivity. The State Government of Assam and the Ministry of Railways collaborated effectively, facilitated by PM GatiShakti, to overcome this challenge and construct the RUB. This project significantly contributed to the overall connectivity strategy.Through the effective coordination facilitated by the PM GatiShakti initiative, the construction of the RUB was
realised successfully, playing a crucial role in the broader connectivity strategy. At the core of the MMLP's development lies its comprehensive strategy. The initiative plans for advanced warehouses and cold storage facilities tailored to accommodate a diverse array of goods, including agricultural produce, fisheries output, and processed commodities that have the potential to boost agriculture, fisheries, and industries. This comprehensive infrastructure further supports local economies and bridges critical infrastructure gaps. Thus, a transition from disjointed connectivity to seamless trade networks showcases the potential of coordinated efforts. The upcoming Dhubri-Phulbari Bridge and the comprehensive facilities within the MMLP reflect the power of strategic execution, underscoring India's dedication to sustainable growth, connectivity, and economic prosperity in the Northeast region.

THE EXPECTED IMPACT

The MMLP will be a critical infrastructure for enabling the efficient movement of goods to and from the Northeast of India. It will boost trade and logistics in the entire Northeast region as well as support India’s aspirations of boosting international trade in Southeast Asia through complementary international road connectivity developed from the Northeast of India.
The total potential demand for capacity at the MMLP is expected to be ~1.332 crores (13.32 million) Metric Tonnes per annum by 2052. It will reduce logistics costs and vehicular emissions by bringing efficiency to long-distance vs. short-distance modes of transportation.
From aggregating the movement of goods to providing better connectivity to economic clusters and across flood-prone areas, along with boosting warehouses and cold storage facilities, the project will enhance both economic and social development.
