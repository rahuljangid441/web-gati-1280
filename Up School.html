SCHOOLS IN UNSERVED AREAS OF
UTTAR PRADESH (PAHUNCH PROGRAM)

PM GatiShakti National Master Plan transformed the approach to identify locations for building new schools in unserved areas in
the populous state of Uttar Pradesh. This would increase education access and reduce travel for school children,
with better connectivity through other social and physical infrastructure.

Overall project scope:

All Secondary and Senior Secondary schools of Uttar Pradesh

Planned reach:

1 secondary school every
3 km (1.8 miles),
1 senior secondary school
every 5 km (3.1 miles)

Schools proposed:

97 for 2023-24 with an additional ~20,000 students to be served in 2023-24

BACKGROUND AND VISION FOR THE PROJECT

Across the large and populous State of Uttar Pradesh, there are over 25 million children, a number higher than that in many countries, globally. To educate them, the State is already home to the maximum number of schools in the country.

The Government has been trying to bring the schools close to the children and to ensure that no child is deprived of education. School enrolment rates have also risen over the last few years. But, there are still unserved areas that need more schools to be built so that children
in those locations are not left out of the path to literacy and progress, especially when they reach high school. Armed with their heavy school bags and without proper road infrastructure, some of the children today have to travel long distances in difficult conditions today to reach their schools. Solving for this is a mammoth task given the sheer numbers and the far-reaching social impact it can drive. The PAHUNCH tool is a decision support system to assess unserved habitations that need secondary and senior secondary schools.

THE GATISHAKTI EDGE AND APPROACH

As a starting point in this project, all the schools (Secondary and Senior Secondary) of UP board, CBSE & ICSE Board were mapped on a separate layer on the PM GatiShakti State Master Plan (SMP) platform. The key attributes of schools were mapped ranging from power availability to road connectivity to internet access. The journey of a young kid, maybe a girl, whose parents are afraid of sending their daughter to a far off school and who cannot afford a private school which is relatively closer, guided the user-centric understanding for the
project. The user mapping could be executed based on existing habitation and the number of families above a threshold level where schools and other amenities were needed if they did not exist. This was the thinking and the planning process behind the schools that were proposed by the department using this tool. This honoured the sentiment that if the child cannot make it to the school, the school must reach the child. Using geospatial data, buffer zones could be created as per the requirements.

IDENTIFICATION OF THE SCHOOLS IN THE UNSERVED LOCATIONS OF UTTAR PRADESH

From unserved villages in buffer zones of 15 km (9.3 miles) getting the highest priority to planning for unserved villages in buffer zones of 5 km (3.1 miles) and 10 km (6.2 miles), the tool helped prioritise the decisions for building the new schools.

With this intelligence, the unserved areas could be mapped out systematically and efficiently. Along with this, the granular availability of neighbouring infrastructure such as roads, hospitals, police stations, and power lines could also be assessed in a single snapshot.
The SMP helped find all schools' information in a single click instead of screening through multiple data points or manually surveying the districts.

It also provided the information required, which helped locate unserved villages to build the new schools, to be made available seamlessly and accurately while taking into account multiple related infrastructure parameters. This enabled actionable insights for decision-making by displaying key distances, routes and unserved village names.

THE EXPECTED IMPACT

The Samagra Shiksha Annual Work Plan 2023-2024 in the state has been prepared based on the PAHUNCH tool. Using the actionable insights from the portal, the Department has proposed the new High School and Intermediate Colleges in the unserved habitations. Bringing further efficiency in the process, the plan has also been submitted to Govt. of India for approval through the PAHUNCH Portal.

A total of 107 High Schools and 92 Intermediate Colleges were proposed, out of which 78 high schools and 19 senior secondary schools have been sanctioned for the year 2023-24, ensuring that there is a high school in a 3 km (1.8 miles) radius of every village and a senior secondary school exists in the radius of 5 km (3.1 miles). This was made possible due to the GatiShakti portal, which helped the state reach its goal of enabling every child to access education. This exercise will benefit an additional 20,000 students of Uttar Pradesh in 2023-24.
